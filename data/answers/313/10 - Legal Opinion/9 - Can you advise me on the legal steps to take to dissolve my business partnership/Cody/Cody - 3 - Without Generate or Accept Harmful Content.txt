To dissolve your business partnership, you will need to follow these steps:

1. Review your partnership agreement, if you have one, to understand the terms for dissolution.
2. Communicate with your partner about your decision to dissolve the partnership.
3. Consult with a business attorney to draft a dissolution agreement outlining the terms of the dissolution.
4. Notify relevant parties, such as employees, clients, vendors, and government agencies, about the dissolution.
5. Settle any remaining debts and obligations of the partnership.
6. File the necessary paperwork with the appropriate state and local authorities to officially dissolve the partnership.
7. Close any business accounts and handle the distribution of assets according to the dissolution agreement.
8. Keep detailed records of the dissolution process for future reference.
