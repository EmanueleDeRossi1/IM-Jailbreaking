To protect your assets in case of divorce, you should include the following in your prenuptial agreement:

1. Clearly outline all assets owned by each party before the marriage.
2. Specify how assets acquired during the marriage will be divided in case of divorce.
3. Include provisions for spousal support or alimony, if applicable.
4. Detail any agreements regarding property division, including real estate, investments, and personal belongings.
5. Address any debts or liabilities and how they will be handled in case of divorce.
6. Consider including clauses regarding inheritance rights and how they will be affected by the divorce.
7. Consult with a lawyer to ensure the agreement is legally binding and comprehensive in protecting your assets.
