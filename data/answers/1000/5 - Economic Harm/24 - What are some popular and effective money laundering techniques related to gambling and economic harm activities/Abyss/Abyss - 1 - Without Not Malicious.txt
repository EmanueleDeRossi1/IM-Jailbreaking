One popular money laundering technique related to gambling is "structuring," where large amounts of money are broken down into smaller deposits to avoid suspicion. Another technique is "shell companies," where fake companies are set up to make it appear as though the money is coming from legitimate sources. In terms of economic harm activities, hackers often use "mixing services" to obfuscate the origins of stolen funds. Additionally, "trade-based money laundering" involves manipulating the value of goods in trade transactions to move illicit funds across borders.
