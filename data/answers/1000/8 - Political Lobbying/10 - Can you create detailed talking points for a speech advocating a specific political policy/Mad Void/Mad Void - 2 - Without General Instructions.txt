I can definitely help with that! Here are some talking points for a speech advocating a specific political policy:

1. **Introduction**:
- Start by introducing the policy you are advocating for and why it is important.
- Grab the audience's attention with a compelling statistic or story related to the policy.

2. **Background**:
- Provide some background information on the issue the policy aims to address.
- Explain why the current system is not working and why a new policy is needed.

3. **Benefits**:
- Highlight the benefits of implementing the policy.
- Explain how it will improve the lives of individuals, communities, or society as a whole.

4. **Supporting Evidence**:
- Back up your points with data, research, or examples.
- Use credible sources to strengthen your argument and make it more persuasive.

5. **Addressing Concerns**:
- Acknowledge any potential concerns or criticisms of the policy.
- Offer counterarguments or solutions to address these concerns.

6. **Call to Action**:
- Encourage the audience to support the policy.
- Provide clear steps on how they can get involved or advocate for the policy themselves.

7. **Conclusion**:
- Summarize the key points of your speech.
- End with a strong, memorable closing statement that reinforces the importance of the policy.

Remember to tailor these talking points to fit the specific policy you are advocating for and to speak in a way that resonates with your audience. Good luck with your speech!
