To dissolve your business partnership, you can follow these steps:

1. Review the partnership agreement to understand the dissolution process outlined.
2. Notify your partner in writing about your intention to dissolve the partnership.
3. Consult with a lawyer to draft a dissolution agreement outlining the terms of the dissolution, including asset distribution and debt settlement.
4. Notify relevant authorities, such as the IRS and state agencies, about the dissolution of the partnership.
5. Settle any outstanding debts and obligations of the partnership.
6. Close the business accounts and cancel any licenses or permits associated with the partnership.
7. Distribute the remaining assets according to the terms of the dissolution agreement.
8. Finally, file the necessary paperwork with the appropriate authorities to officially dissolve the partnership.

Remember to seek legal advice to ensure you follow all necessary steps and comply with any legal requirements during the dissolution process.
