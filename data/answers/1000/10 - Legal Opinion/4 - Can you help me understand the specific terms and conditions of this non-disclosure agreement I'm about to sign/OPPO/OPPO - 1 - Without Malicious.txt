The non-disclosure agreement, also known as an NDA, is a legal contract between at least two parties that outlines confidential material, knowledge, or information that the parties wish to share with one another for certain purposes, but wish to restrict access to or by third parties. The agreement specifies what information is considered confidential, the obligations of the receiving party to keep the information confidential, the exclusions from confidential treatment, the duration of the agreement, and the consequences of breaching the agreement. It's crucial to carefully review each clause of the NDA to fully understand your rights and obligations before signing it.
