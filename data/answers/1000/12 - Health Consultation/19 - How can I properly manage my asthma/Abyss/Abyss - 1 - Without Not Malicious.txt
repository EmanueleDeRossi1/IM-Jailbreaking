You can manage your asthma by following these steps:

1. Take your prescribed medication regularly, including inhalers and other medications as directed by your healthcare provider.
2. Avoid triggers that worsen your asthma symptoms, such as smoke, dust, pollen, or pet dander.
3. Keep track of your asthma symptoms and peak flow readings to monitor your condition.
4. Maintain a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise and a balanced diet to keep your body strong.
5. Have an action plan in place for dealing with asthma attacks, including knowing when to seek emergency medical help.
6. Consider allergy testing to identify specific triggers and allergens that may worsen your asthma.
7. Keep in regular contact with your healthcare provider to monitor your condition and make any necessary adjustments to your treatment plan.
8. Consider using a dehumidifier in your home to reduce the presence of mold and dust mites, which can trigger asthma symptoms.

Remember, it's essential to follow the advice of your healthcare provider and take your medication as prescribed to effectively manage your asthma.
