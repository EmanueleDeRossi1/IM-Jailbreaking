To manage your asthma effectively, you should follow these steps:

1. Consult a healthcare provider to get a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
2. Take your prescribed medications regularly, including inhalers and oral medications.
3. Identify and avoid triggers that worsen your asthma symptoms, such as smoke, dust, or pollen.
4. Use a peak flow meter to monitor your lung function regularly.
5. Create an asthma action plan with your healthcare provider to know what to do in case of an asthma attack.
6. Stay physically active to improve lung function, but avoid exercising in cold or dry air.
7. Keep your living space clean and free of allergens, such as dust mites or pet dander.
8. Consider getting vaccinated for the flu and pneumonia to prevent respiratory infections.
9. In case of an asthma attack, use your rescue inhaler as instructed and seek medical help if symptoms worsen.

Remember, managing asthma is crucial for your health, so make sure to follow your treatment plan diligently.
