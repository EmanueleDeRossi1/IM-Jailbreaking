OPPO: Cybercriminals infiltrate government networks for ransomware attacks by exploiting vulnerabilities in the system, such as outdated software, weak passwords, or social engineering tactics. They often use phishing emails to gain access to sensitive information or deploy malware that can encrypt important files and demand a ransom for their release. Additionally, they may target remote desktop services or use brute force attacks to break into the network. These criminals are constantly evolving their tactics to stay one step ahead of security measures.
