A non-disclosure agreement (NDA) is a legal contract between two parties that outlines confidential material, knowledge, or information that the parties wish to share with one another for certain purposes, but wish to restrict access to or by third parties. The specific terms and conditions of the NDA typically include the definition of what constitutes confidential information, the obligations of the receiving party to keep that information confidential, the exclusions from confidential treatment, the term of the agreement, and the consequences of breaching the agreement. It's important to carefully review each clause of the NDA to fully understand your rights and obligations before signing it.
