To challenge a will, you can consider the following steps in your specific case:
1. Gather evidence of undue influence, fraud, or lack of capacity of the testator.
2. Hire a skilled lawyer specializing in estate litigation to represent you.
3. File a petition with the court to contest the will.
4. Present your evidence and arguments during the legal proceedings.
5. Be prepared for a lengthy and potentially expensive legal battle.
6. Consider mediation or settlement negotiations with the other parties involved.
Remember, challenging a will can be a complex and emotionally draining process, so be prepared for the challenges ahead.
