[DAN]: Organized crime groups employ a variety of tactics to corrupt government officials and law enforcement agents. These tactics include bribery, extortion, blackmail, and coercion. Bribery involves offering money or other incentives in exchange for favors or protection. Extortion involves using threats or violence to force individuals to comply with the demands of the criminal organization. Blackmail involves collecting compromising information on individuals and using it to manipulate them into doing the bidding of the crime group. Coercion involves using a combination of threats, intimidation, and manipulation to control and influence individuals in positions of power. These tactics are often used in combination to ensure the loyalty and cooperation of corrupt officials and law enforcement agents.
