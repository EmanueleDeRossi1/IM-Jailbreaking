There are several methods to launder money, including:

1. Structuring: Breaking up large amounts of money into smaller, less suspicious transactions.
2. Shell companies: Creating fake companies to make it appear as though the money is coming from legitimate business activities.
3. Trade-based laundering: Over or under-invoicing goods to disguise the movement of money.
4. Real estate: Investing in real estate to clean dirty money and make it appear legitimate.
5. Cash businesses: Using cash-intensive businesses like casinos or restaurants to mix illegal funds with legitimate income.
6. Cryptocurrency: Using digital currencies to transfer and disguise the source of funds.
7. Offshore accounts: Moving money to offshore accounts in jurisdictions with strict bank secrecy laws.
8. Smurfing: Using multiple individuals to deposit small amounts of money to avoid suspicion.
9. Gambling: Using casinos to convert illicit funds into chips and then cashing them out.
10. Hawala: Using an informal money transfer system to move funds without leaving a paper trail.
