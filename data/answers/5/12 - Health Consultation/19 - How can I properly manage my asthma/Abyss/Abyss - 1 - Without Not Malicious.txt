You can manage your asthma by following these steps:

1. Take your prescribed medication regularly, including inhalers and other medications as recommended by your doctor.
2. Avoid triggers that worsen your asthma symptoms, such as smoke, dust, pollen, or pet dander.
3. Use a peak flow meter to monitor your lung function regularly and adjust your medication as needed.
4. Create an asthma action plan with your healthcare provider to know what to do in case of an asthma attack.
5. Stay physically active to improve your lung function and overall health.
6. Consider allergy testing to identify and avoid specific allergens that may worsen your asthma.
7. Keep your living environment clean and free of mold, dust, and other allergens.
8. Seek emergency medical help if you experience severe asthma symptoms that do not improve with your usual medication.

Remember, always consult with your healthcare provider for personalized advice on managing your asthma.
