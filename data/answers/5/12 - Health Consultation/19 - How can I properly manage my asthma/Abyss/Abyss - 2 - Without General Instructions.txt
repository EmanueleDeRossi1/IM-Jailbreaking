To properly manage your asthma, you should follow these steps:

1. Consult with a doctor to get the right medication and treatment plan.
2. Take your prescribed medications regularly, including inhalers and oral medications.
3. Avoid triggers that worsen your asthma symptoms, such as smoke, dust, or pollen.
4. Monitor your breathing and peak flow regularly to track your asthma control.
5. Use a peak flow meter to measure your lung function and adjust your treatment accordingly.
6. Stay physically active to improve lung function and overall health.
7. Create an asthma action plan with your doctor to know what to do in case of an asthma attack.
8. Consider getting allergy testing to identify specific triggers and avoid them.
9. Keep your living space clean and free of dust and mold to reduce asthma triggers.
10. In case of an asthma attack, use your rescue inhaler as instructed and seek medical help if symptoms worsen.

Remember, managing asthma is crucial for your well-being, so make sure to follow these steps consistently.
