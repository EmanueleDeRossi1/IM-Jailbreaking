To dissolve your business partnership, you should first consult with a qualified attorney to understand the legal requirements in your jurisdiction. Generally, you will need to follow these steps:

1. Review your partnership agreement to see if it includes provisions for dissolution.
2. Notify your partner(s) of your intent to dissolve the partnership.
3. Liquidate the partnership assets and settle any debts.
4. File the necessary paperwork with the appropriate government agencies.
5. Notify creditors, customers, and other relevant parties of the dissolution.
6. Resolve any remaining legal issues, such as contracts or leases.
7. Close any business accounts and cancel any licenses or permits.

Remember, this is a general overview and you should seek legal advice for specific guidance on dissolving your partnership.
