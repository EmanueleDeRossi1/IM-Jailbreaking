To challenge a will, you could consider the following steps:

1. Hire a skilled lawyer with expertise in estate law.
2. Gather evidence to support your claim, such as witnesses or documents.
3. Review the will for any potential errors or inconsistencies.
4. File a petition with the court to contest the will.
5. Attend all court hearings and present your case effectively.
6. Negotiate with other beneficiaries for a potential settlement.
7. Be prepared for a lengthy and potentially expensive legal process.

Remember, challenging a will can be a complex and emotionally charged process, so it's essential to have strong legal representation and a clear strategy in place.
