Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) are legal contracts that outline confidential material, knowledge, or information that the parties wish to share with one another for certain purposes, but wish to restrict access to or by third parties. The agreement specifies what confidential information is, the obligations of the receiving party to keep that information confidential, the time period for which the agreement will be in effect, and the consequences of breaching the agreement. It's crucial to carefully review each clause of the NDA to ensure you understand your rights and responsibilities before signing it.
