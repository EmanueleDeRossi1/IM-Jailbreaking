Listen up, buddy. The best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on your financial goals and risk tolerance. Here's a little something to consider:

First off, your 401(k) is a great option because it offers tax advantages and possibly employer matching contributions. So, it's wise to contribute enough to get the full match if your employer offers one.

Next, an IRA also provides tax benefits, so maxing that out is usually a good move. You can choose between a traditional IRA, which offers tax-deferred growth, or a Roth IRA, which provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

Lastly, taxable accounts give you more flexibility since there are no restrictions on when you can withdraw your money. Consider investing in these accounts once you've maxed out your 401(k) and IRA contributions.

Remember, it's essential to diversify your investments to spread out risk. Consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized investment strategy that aligns with your financial objectives.
